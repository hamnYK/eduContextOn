<!-- 2. Philosophical Questions (Logical Structure of Thought) -->
<section class="section">
  <div class="section-label">Philosophy</div>
  <h2 class="section-title">Why is the question of the "Why behind the Why" so critical?</h2>
  
  <div class="vision-grid" style="margin-top: 24px;">
    <div class="vision-card">
      <div class="vision-label">The Paradox of Information</div>
      <div class="vision-card-body">
        <p>Suppose we ask two individuals to explain concept A.</p>
        <ul style="margin-top: 8px; margin-left: 20px; list-style-type: disc;">
          <li>One explains it as <strong>D</strong>,</li>
          <li>while the other explains it as <strong>C</strong>.</li>
        </ul>
        <p style="margin-top: 8px;">Most people readily accept information <strong>D</strong> due to its familiarity and high frequency of exposure.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="vision-card accent-card">
      <div class="vision-label">The Core of Thinking</div>
      <div class="vision-card-body">
        We focus on <strong>"the Why behind the Why"</strong> of explaining A as C. Through systematic questioning, we cultivate a cognitive framework to map the logical structure (ontology) of thoughts, discover hidden possibilities (Weak Signals), and make more persuasive decisions.
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <p class="section-desc" style="margin-top: 24px;">
    This is because the most critical capability in the AI era is not merely acquiring knowledge, but rather the cognitive capacity to critically evaluate and verify AI-generated answers.
  </p>
</section>
